## Verdict: NOT-APPLICABLE (no digitizable content) — digitization correctly absent

Figure 1 of Heeger (1992) is a **schematic architecture diagram**, not a data
figure. The paper panel shows: a STIMULUS row at top, two linear-operator
channels drawn as circles subdivided into bright (excitatory) / dark
(inhibitory) subregions, half-squaring/energy stages, per-channel summation
('+') nodes, simple-cell outputs S_i^phi, a complex-cell output C_i, and a
divisive-feedback signal entering from the side. There are **no quantitative
axes, no curves, no scatter, no heatmap, no histogram** — nothing to digitize.

The caption itself ("Diagram of the various stages of the model") and
`figure_1.md` ("Schematic figure (no computation). The reproduction target is
the spatial LAYOUT") confirm the content type. The reproduction target is
spatial structure (element-above-element claims), graded by the visual checklist
in `figure_1_visual_checklist.md`, not by a digitized reference.

## What I checked

1. **Is there a digitized reference to audit?** No. Searched the whole model
   tree: there is **no** `article_aware/figures/figure_1/panel_*_digitized.json`
   (and no `figure_1/` panel-crop directory at all). No `*_digitized.json`
   exists anywhere in `models/heeger_1992`. This absence is **correct**, not a
   finding: a schematic has no curve/point/axis content for the digitize-figure
   tool to extract.

2. **Is the figure genuinely non-digitizable, or is digitization being dodged?**
   Genuinely non-digitizable. I read the paper PNG directly (rendered above in
   the audit session). It is a node-and-arrow circuit diagram. Per the skill's
   own taxonomy, content with "no available tool" / no quantitative structure is
   handled by deferral, not by eyeballing a curve out of a diagram. A category
   error would be the reverse — "digitizing" a diagram into a handful of fake
   curve points. That did **not** happen here; no such JSON was fabricated.

3. **Is the schematic reproduction faithful to the paper layout?** (Sanity, not
   this skill's core remit — that belongs to the visual-checklist /
   faithfulness gate.) Comparing `figures_reproduced/figure_1.png` to the paper:
   stimulus at top, two channels with bright/dark split circles, '+' summation
   nodes below the circles, S_i^phi labels below the sums, C_i below and between
   the simple-cell outputs, feedback node entering from the side into the
   summation nodes. The layout matches the paper's staged pipeline and the
   divisive-feedback topology. No layout divergence observed at panel scale.

## Note / limitation

I read the committed `figures_reproduced/figure_1.png` rather than re-running the
view render (Step 0 freshness). For a *data* figure that would be a gap, since a
stale render can mask a calibration/PCHIP drift. For this **schematic** the only

cannot hide a digitization error (there is no digitization). If the organizer
wants Step-0 strictness regardless, re-render via

does not change this verdict.
