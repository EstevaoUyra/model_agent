## What the panels are (per `figure_3.md`)

Four panels, each three SMOOTH fitted psychometric curves
`d′(c)=d′max·cⁿ/(cⁿ+c50ⁿ)`, valid(red)/neutral(black)/invalid(blue), log-x contrast %,
linear-y d′ (0–3). The **smooth fitted curves are the reproduction target**; the
empirical mean±s.e.m. scatter dots and significance brackets are explicitly out-of-scope

(c50 shifts, d′max n.s.).

## Per-panel verdict

### Binding dimensions that ARE faithful (all four panels)

- **Axis calibration** — sound. Overlaid curves land on the printed fitted ink: feet at
  d′≈0, decade ticks (10,100) aligned, plateaus at the correct printed heights. y anchored
  on the printed 0/1/2/3 digit ticks (~73 px/unit), x on the decade ticks. No log-as-linear
  / offset-origin / swapped-range error.
- **Plateau heights (d′max)** — match the paper: 3a valid≈2.68/neutral≈2.10/invalid≈1.42;
  3b ≈2.55/2.30/1.91; 3c/3d a common high plateau (~2.4–2.7) — the contrast-gain "d′max
  n.s." signature. No per-panel auto-normalize-to-1.0; the shared 0–3 d′ scale is preserved
  across all four panels (the cross-panel claim is intact).
- **Curve count / colour→condition mapping / completeness** — correct: three curves per
  panel, red=valid, black=neutral, blue=invalid, ordering and the regime signature
  (response-gain height-separation in a,b; contrast-gain horizontal c50 shift in c,d) all
  present and correct.

### DIGITIZATION-DIVERGENT — panels 3b and 3c, **minor→major** (flank not smooth)

The stored points on panels **b and c** are still **jagged on the rising flank** — the
tracer was pulled toward the out-of-scope empirical mean±s.e.m. scatter dots and never
de-kinked, so the digitized "fitted curve" carries step-jumps / PCHIP wiggles that the
paper's analytic sigmoid does not have. This is the *exact* defect the digitizer diagnosed
and fixed on panels a and d — but only on a and d.

Evidence (max |2nd-difference| of the stored JSON points = curvature jaggedness; the
paper's fitted curve is analytically smooth so this should be small and uniform):

| panel | de-kinked? | valid | neutral | invalid |
|-------|-----------|-------|---------|---------|
| a | yes (savgol + `defect_fix`) | 0.024 | 0.017 | **0.015** |
| d | yes (savgol + `defect_fix`) | 0.024 | 0.029 | 0.023 |
| **b** | **no** | 0.136 | 0.092 | 0.128 |
| **c** | **no** | 0.141 | 0.173 | **0.196** |

Panels b/c are **~5–10× jaggier** than the de-kinked a/d. Visually (see
`.agent_tmp/fig3_audit/c_flank.png`, `b_flank.png`): the 3c neutral and 3c invalid
overlays wiggle (an S-shaped dip-and-rise) through the empirical dots on the mid-flank
where the printed blue/black fitted ink is a clean smooth sigmoid; the 3b neutral/invalid
upper flank shows the same milder kinks. In the raw 3c JSON the jumps are explicit, e.g.
neutral `…0.706 → 0.907 → 0.935…` and invalid `…0.479 → 0.675 → 0.675 → 0.753…` between
adjacent log-contrast samples — adjacent-sample steps up to ~0.22 d′, against a smooth fit.

- **Severity:** minor on the macro claim (d′max, c50, ordering, signature all intact and
  faithful), but **major for its job as the ruler.** The three-tier figure tests grade the
  smooth model curve against this reference; a jagged reference will (i) impose spurious
  local flank error a faithful smooth model cannot match under a tightened tier-3 tolerance,
  and (ii) bake the out-of-scope empirical scatter into the "fitted-curve" target. The
  digitizer's own provenance asserts the fix preserves the curve while removing exactly
  these step-jumps — it just wasn't applied to b and c.
- **Refute pass:** survives. The jaggedness is computed on the stored points themselves
  (independent of my overlay's PCHIP), the caption/`figure_3.md` make the target an
  analytic smooth sigmoid, the printed ink is visibly smooth, and the digitizer already
  treated this same pattern as a defect on a/d.

### TOOL-MISUSE (same root cause) — panels 3b, 3c

The provenance confirms the asymmetry: panels **a** and **d** list the `savgol_filter
(de-kink low-pass)` tool and carry a `defect_fix` block; panels **b** and **c** list
neither and were left at the bare-tracer output. So b/c are the "tracer pulled by the
out-of-scope empirical scatter" eyeball-adjacent tell the skill names — the right tool
(the de-kink low-pass) existed and was used on the sibling panels but **not** applied here.
Process finding in its own right: the de-kink step that defines the fitted-curve target
was applied inconsistently across the four panels of the same figure.
