# Digitization Audit — Heeger 1992, Figure 2

- **Date:** 2026-06-03
- **Auditor role:** figure-digitization critic (audit-digitization). NOT the digitizer.
- **Target:** `models/heeger_1992` — Figure 2, **panel B** (model contrast-response
  family, one curve per sigma). Panel A is empirical (Ohzawa 1985) and not a model
  reproduction target; it is out of scope here beyond context.
- **Inputs read:** paper figure `article_aware/figures/figure_2.png` + `figure_2.md`;
  digitized JSON `figure_2/panel_b_digitized.json` (incl. `provenance`); panel crop
  `panel_b_crop.png`; shipping overlay `panel_b_overlay.png`. Re-derived calibration and
  re-cropped the paper plateau region myself (crop_region, upscale 5-6) rather than
  trusting committed renders.

## Verdict

**Panel 2B: DIGITIZATION-DIVERGENT (major).** The reference is well-calibrated and the
feet / low-contrast rise / sigma=0.5 curve are faithful, but **the plateau region of the
top four curves is corrupted by tracer-merge**: multiple curves share *identical* y-values
at the same x-columns and four of five curves are **non-monotone** (dips/wiggles) in the
saturation region. The shipping overlay reproduces the defect visibly — the sigma=0.031
(red) rendered line sags off the topmost square markers in the mid-plateau. The provenance
explicitly claims this bunching is "faithful, not a tracer merge"; the data and the paper
crop contradict that claim.

## What is faithful (state explicitly)

- **Axis calibration** — sound. x: 1/10/100 % at cols 84.5/222.5/360 (~137.75 px/decade,
  log); y: 1/.1/.01 at rows 88/223/358 (~135 px/decade, log). Corner sanity and my
  independent re-crop agree. The y-axis box sitting at ~1.6% contrast (not the x=1 tick) is
  handled correctly.
- **Normalization** — correct and notable. The reference is recorded **as drawn**: top
  curve plateaus ~0.74 (NOT 1.0), shared single y-scale across all five curves, no
  per-panel auto-normalization to 1.0. This matches analytic R(c=1)=k/(sigma^2+1) ~ 0.70-0.74
  for the top four curves with k~0.74, and matches the paper's top-right corner where the
  top curve sits below the y=1 gridline. This is the trap the skill warns about and it was
  avoided. Good.
- **Feet, low-contrast rise, lateral shift, sigma=0.5** — faithful. Cross-checking every
  point against the exact model R = k*c^2/(sigma^2+c^2) (k=0.74): for sigma=0.5 all 14
  points are within ~0.02 of analytic; feet of all curves within ~0.01; the lateral-shift
  family (smaller sigma = leftmost/highest) is correct. The leftward floor handling
  (sigma=0.25, 0.5 off-scale below .01, intentionally not plotted) is correct.
- **Completeness/mapping** — all five sigma present, correct color/marker -> sigma
  assignment, right count.

## Findings (severity-ordered)

### F1 — Tracer-merge in the plateau: identical points shared across curves (major)
Several plateau points are byte-identical across 2-4 curves, i.e. the continuity-follow
collapsed the bunched curves onto ONE shared trace rather than separating them:

| shared y | curves (x %) |
|----------|--------------|
| 0.6522 | sigma=0.031, 0.0625, 0.125 all at x=37.055 |
| 0.6625 | sigma=0.031, 0.0625, 0.125 all at x=50.711 |
| 0.6935 | sigma=0.031, 0.0625, 0.125 all at x=27.076 |
| 0.711  | sigma=0.031, 0.0625, 0.125 at x=69.4 **and sigma=0.25 at x=55.917** |
| 0.7356 | sigma=0.031, 0.0625, 0.125, 0.25 all at x=94.976 |
| 0.5963 | sigma=0.031 and sigma=0.0625 both at x=14.457 |

Identical floating-point values at the same columns are not "faithful convergence" — they
are the tracer reading the same pixels for different curves. The paper plateau (my
crop_region zoom, x 8-100%, y 0.4-1.0) shows the curves remain **separated and monotone**
through the mid-plateau (x~14-56%); they only become genuinely near-coincident at the
extreme top-right corner (x > ~70%). The merge pulls the lower curves up onto the top line
in a region where the paper keeps them apart.

### F2 — Non-monotone curves (dips/wiggles) — overlay shows it (major)
The data is non-monotone in 4 of 5 curves (Naka-Rushton must be strictly increasing):
- sigma=0.031: 0.6473 (x=10.6) -> **0.5963** (x=14.5) -> 0.6698 (x=19.8); and 0.6935 -> 0.6522.
- sigma=0.0625, sigma=0.125: 0.6935 (x=27.1) -> 0.6522 (x=37.1) dip.
- sigma=0.25: 0.711 (x=55.9) -> 0.6822 (x=72.9) dip.

The sigma=0.031 point [14.457, 0.5963] is -0.11 below analytic (the largest single error in
the panel) and is identical to sigma=0.0625 at the same x — the top curve's trace dropped
onto the curve below it. **This is visible in the SHIPPING overlay**: the red (sigma=0.031)
PCHIP line rises onto the topmost filled-square markers, then **sags down below the top
square row and rejoins** — a kink the paper's flat, monotone top-square curve does not have
(see `panel_b_overlay.png` mid-plateau; my zoom `overlay_reddip` makes the sag unambiguous).
A clean re-trace of the data would not fix this because the *data itself* dips.

### F3 — sigma=0.25 merged into the plateau cluster at x~56% (major)
Digitized [55.917, 0.711] vs analytic 0.617 (+0.094). My crop (x 40-100, y 0.3-0.85) shows
the sigma=0.25 open-circle curve at x~56% still clearly rising around y~0.55-0.60, well below
the top plateau cluster. The digitized point grabbed the plateau line. Same root cause as
F1/F2.

### F4 — Provenance overclaims faithfulness of the bunching (process / minor-but-relevant)
`provenance.caveats` states: "Top-right convergence ... bunching is faithful, not a tracer
merge; ... Verified by overlay." The identical shared y-values (F1), the non-monotone dips
(F2), and the overlay sag (F2) contradict this. The self-overlay check passed a merged,
wiggling plateau as faithful — the exact builder-grades-own-homework failure this role
exists to catch. The tool set (detect_plot_box / build_calibration / trace continuity-follow
/ resample_pchip / overlay / crop_region) is appropriate for a Mode-1 quantitative plot; the
documented same-color-curve limitation of `trace_darkest_in_band` was acknowledged but **not
actually resolved** in the plateau — the continuity-follow seam where the five same-color
curves bunch is exactly where it failed.

## Refute pass
- Could the red sag / dips be a faithful paper feature? No — the paper's top filled-square
  row (crop_region zooms `f2_plateau`, `f2_plateau_height`, `f2_topright`) is flat and
  monotone; Naka-Rushton is analytically monotone; the dip coincides with byte-identical
  shared points -> artifact, not feature. **Survives.**
- Could the shared plateau values be genuine coincidence (R(c=1)=k/(sigma^2+1) ~ 0.70-0.74 for
  the top three)? At the **extreme** top-right corner (x>70%), near-coincidence IS faithful
  (kept that part of the provenance claim). But the merges at x=27-56% are 0.04-0.11 above
  analytic and the paper keeps the curves separated there -> the *mid-plateau* merges
  **survive** as defects; the top-right-corner coincidence is dismissed (faithful).
- Normalization-to-1.0 trap? Checked — NOT present; reference faithfully plateaus ~0.74.

## Disposition (for the organizer — I do not gate)
Panel 2B carries unresolved **DIGITIZATION-DIVERGENT (major)**: the digitized reference is
**not-yet-binding** for the three-tier figure tests in the plateau region. The feet /
low-contrast rise / lateral-shift / normalization are sound and could anchor those parts of
the tests, but the saturation region must be re-traced so each same-color curve is followed
independently (no shared points), enforcing monotonicity, before the plateau is graded
against. Because this is an exact-formula model panel (Eq. 7, n=2, M=0), the cleanest fix is
to regenerate the reference plateau from R = k*c^2/(sigma^2+c^2) with k~0.74 and verify
against the markers, rather than hand-trace the bunched ink.
